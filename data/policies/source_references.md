# Source References and Data Disclaimer

The Acme Corp policy documents in this repository are **fictional** and created for demonstration purposes only. **Acme Corp is not a real company.**

They are original synthetic policies inspired by common structures found in publicly available guidance and enterprise practice, including:

- **IRS accountable-plan style reimbursement principles** — business connection, substantiation, timely submission, receipts, and expense reports.
- **Public travel and expense policy patterns** — separation of alcohol, itemization, receipts, business purpose, and approval thresholds.
- **Anti-bribery and gifts/hospitality guidance** — cash-equivalent prohibitions, public officials, transparency, proportionality, gift registers, and conflict-of-interest controls.
- **NIST access-control and least-privilege principles** — need-to-know access, role-based controls, and periodic access review.
- **Customer-information safeguarding concepts** — limiting external sharing, vendor review, and incident reporting.
- **Remote work guidance** — eligibility, medical accommodations, secure workspace, cross-border tax/immigration/permanent-establishment risk, and approval workflows.

**No real company confidential policy text is included. No public source text is copied verbatim.**

The purpose of these documents is to provide a realistic mock corpus for testing a RAG-based policy assistant and the PolicyOps Agent workflow.

**Important:** These policies are not legal, HR, finance, tax, or compliance advice. Do not use them for real workplace decisions.
