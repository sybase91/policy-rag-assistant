# Acme Corp Remote Work Policy

Policy Owner: People Operations  
Effective Date: 2026-01-01  
Version: 2.0  
Applies To: All Acme Corp employees  
Related Policies: Approval Matrix, Data Access Policy  
Systems of Record: HR Workflow, Manager Approval Portal

## RW-001 Purpose

This policy defines when employees may work remotely, what approvals are required, and what security and availability obligations apply.

## RW-002 Standard Remote Work

Occasional work-from-home for convenience requires manager awareness but not formal approval for a single day. Employees must remain reachable during core hours.

## RW-003 Extended Remote Work

Remote work for more than 5 consecutive business days and up to 20 business days requires manager approval and a documented plan for availability and deliverables.

## RW-004 Medical Exceptions

Medical-related remote work for more than 5 consecutive business days should be supported by appropriate documentation when requested and routed to HR for review. Managers must not collect detailed medical records.

## RW-005 Cross-Border Remote Work

Working remotely from outside the employee's assigned country requires HR, Legal, Tax, and Information Security approval in advance. Default maximum is 10 business days per calendar year unless a broader arrangement is approved. Cross-border work may create payroll, immigration, social security, employment law, and permanent-establishment risk.

## RW-006 Data Security During Remote Work

Employees working remotely must use approved devices, VPN, secure networks, and follow Acme Corp data access rules. Public or shared devices and unsecured networks are not permitted for Confidential or Restricted data.

## RW-007 Equipment and Stipends

Standard remote work does not include reimbursement for personal furniture or internet unless covered by a separate stipend program.

## RW-008 Attendance Expectations

Employees must remain reachable during core business hours unless an alternate schedule is approved by their manager.

## RW-009 Escalation

Disputes about remote work eligibility escalate to HR and the employee's management chain.

## RW-010 Scope

This policy applies to occasional, short-term, extended, hybrid, medical, and cross-border remote work arrangements.

## RW-011 Definitions Occasional and Short-Term Remote Work

Occasional remote work means a single day or infrequent ad hoc days. Short-term remote work means 2–5 consecutive business days.

## RW-012 Definitions Extended and Hybrid Work

Extended remote work means more than 5 consecutive business days. Recurring hybrid work means a repeating pattern such as fixed remote days each week.

## RW-013 Definitions Medical and Cross-Border

Medical accommodation remote work is requested for health-related needs. Cross-border remote work means performing work outside the assigned country.

## RW-014 Eligibility

Eligibility depends on role suitability, performance standing, data and security constraints, and client or site obligations.

## RW-015 Short-Term Approval Requirements

| Duration | Approval |
|----------|----------|
| One-off day | Manager awareness |
| 2–5 business days | Manager approval |
| 6–20 business days | Manager approval + documented work plan |
| More than 20 business days | Manager + HR approval |

## RW-016 Medical Accommodation Process

HR may request appropriate documentation. Managers coordinate schedule and coverage without collecting sensitive medical details.

## RW-017 Cross-Border Risk Controls

Cross-border arrangements require review of tax, payroll, immigration, and data residency before approval.

## RW-018 Availability and Time Zones

Employees must define core hours, meeting availability, and response expectations in the documented work plan.

## RW-019 Workspace Safety

Employees must maintain a safe, professional workspace and report injuries or ergonomic concerns to HR.

## RW-020 Expenses Not Reimbursable

Personal furniture, home internet outside stipend programs, and non-approved equipment are generally not reimbursable.

## RW-021 Performance and Attendance

Remote work does not change performance standards or attendance expectations unless formally approved.

## RW-022 Revocation of Remote Work

Acme may revoke remote work arrangements for performance, security, business, or role-change reasons.

## RW-023 Secure Workspace Requirements

Restricted data must not be discussed or displayed in public places. Printing Restricted data at home requires approval.

## RW-024 High-Risk Location or Network

Working from high-risk locations or networks requires Information Security review.

## RW-025 Systems of Record

Approvals and work plans must be stored in the HR workflow or manager approval portal.

## RW-026 Prohibited Actions

Employees must not use personal devices or public networks for Restricted data without approval.

## RW-027 Exceptions

Exceptions require HR and, where relevant, Legal or InfoSec approval.

## RW-028 Related Policies

Employees must also follow the Data Access Policy and Approval Matrix for all remote work arrangements.

## RW-029 Audit and Monitoring

HR and InfoSec may audit remote work approvals and security compliance.

## RW-030 Policy Summary for Employees

In summary: single-day remote work needs manager awareness; multi-day remote work needs documented approval; medical and cross-border cases need HR and additional review; all remote work must follow data security rules.

### Example: One remote day

Scenario: Employee works from home one day for convenience.  
Policy outcome: Allowed with manager awareness.  
Required approvals: Manager awareness.  
Relevant sections: RW-002, AM-011.

### Example: Two weeks with manager approval

Scenario: Employee works remotely for two weeks and already has manager approval.  
Policy outcome: Allowed with documented approval.  
Required approvals: Manager (already obtained).  
Why: Two weeks falls in the 6–20 business day band.  
Relevant sections: RW-003, RW-015, AM-011.

### Example: Two weeks medical reason

Scenario: Employee requests two weeks remote work for a medical reason.  
Policy outcome: Needs approval.  
Required approvals: Manager, HR review.  
Why: Medical exception over 5 days requires HR review.  
Relevant sections: RW-004, RW-016, AM-011.

### Example: One month abroad

Scenario: Employee wants to work from another country for one month.  
Policy outcome: Escalate / Needs approval.  
Required approvals: HR, Legal, Tax, InfoSec.  
Why: Cross-border remote work requires advance multi-functional approval.  
Relevant sections: RW-005, RW-017, AM-011.

### Example: Working from cafe on customer data

Scenario: Employee works from a cafe and accesses customer data.  
Policy outcome: Not allowed.  
Why: Public network and location are not secure for customer data.  
Relevant sections: RW-006, RW-023, DA-004.

### Example: Recurring Fridays remote

Scenario: Employee wants every Friday remote.  
Policy outcome: Needs approval.  
Required approvals: Manager; HR if extended pattern exceeds thresholds.  
Relevant sections: RW-012, RW-015.

### Example: Equipment reimbursement

Scenario: Employee requests reimbursement for a home desk.  
Policy outcome: Not allowed unless stipend program applies.  
Relevant sections: RW-007, RW-020.

### Example: Timezone shift

Scenario: Employee temporarily works from another timezone.  
Policy outcome: Needs approval with documented availability plan.  
Relevant sections: RW-018, RW-015.
