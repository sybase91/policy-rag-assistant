# Acme Corp Gifts and Hospitality Policy

Policy Owner: Legal and Compliance Team  
Effective Date: 2026-01-01  
Version: 2.0  
Applies To: All Acme Corp employees, contractors, and their family members where conduct affects Acme  
Related Policies: Approval Matrix, Travel and Expense, Data Access  
Systems of Record: Acme Gift Register, Compliance Workflow

## GH-001 Purpose

This policy governs giving and receiving gifts, hospitality, entertainment, and business courtesies to prevent bribery, conflicts of interest, and compliance risk. Acme Corp applies a proportionate, transparent, and documented approach inspired by common anti-bribery and hospitality controls.

## GH-002 Accepting Gifts

Employees may accept modest business courtesies that are infrequent, transparent, documented when required, and cannot reasonably influence business decisions. Gifts must comply with threshold, reporting, and approval rules in this policy.

## GH-003 Gift Value Thresholds

| Value band | Requirement |
|------------|-------------|
| Under INR 2,500 | Generally acceptable if modest, infrequent, not cash, not from a public official, and not during a procurement blackout |
| INR 2,500–5,000 | Manager awareness recommended |
| Above INR 5,000 | Report to Compliance and record in the gift register |
| Above INR 10,000 | Manager and Compliance approval required before acceptance |
| Cumulative above INR 15,000 from same counterparty in rolling 12 months | Compliance review |

Gifts from vendors or clients above INR 5,000 must be reported to Compliance. Gifts above INR 10,000 require manager and Compliance approval before acceptance.

## GH-004 Cash and Cash Equivalents

Cash, gift cards, vouchers, cryptocurrency, prepaid cards, loans, and personal discounts are not allowed from vendors, clients, or partners unless explicitly approved under a formal Acme program. Cash-equivalent rewards must be treated as high risk.

## GH-005 Giving Gifts to Clients

Employees giving gifts to clients must keep values reasonable, document business purpose, and obtain manager approval for gifts above INR 5,000.

## GH-006 Gift Register

Gifts above INR 5,000 must be recorded in the Acme Corp gift register within 5 business days. Public-official-related items must be registered regardless of value. Refused gifts above the reporting threshold should also be recorded when practical.

## GH-007 Public Officials

Gifts or hospitality involving public officials require Legal and Compliance pre-approval before offer or acceptance. Employees should generally avoid gifts to public officials unless clearly lawful, modest, transparent, and documented.

## GH-008 Conflicts of Interest

Employees must decline gifts that create or appear to create a conflict of interest, including repeated or escalating offers from the same counterparty.

## GH-009 Escalation

Compliance must review any gift or hospitality request involving unusual value, repeated offers, sensitive counterparties, procurement timing, or public officials.

## GH-010 Scope

This policy applies to gifts and hospitality involving vendors, clients, partners, government officials, conferences, and third-party events.

## GH-011 Definitions Gift

A gift is any item of value offered or received without full market payment, including merchandise, tickets, services, or personal benefits.

## GH-012 Definitions Hospitality and Entertainment

Hospitality includes meals, events, or entertainment offered in a business context. Entertainment includes tickets, outings, or leisure activities where business purpose must be documented.

## GH-013 Definitions Cash Equivalent and Public Official

Cash equivalent means anything readily convertible to cash or personal financial benefit. Public official includes government employees, regulators, and others acting in an official capacity.

## GH-014 Definitions Vendor Client and Procurement Blackout

Vendor means any current or prospective supplier. Client means a current or prospective customer. Procurement blackout means active RFP, contract negotiation, renewal, audit, dispute, or vendor selection.

## GH-015 Modest Courtesies

Low-value branded items, working meals with business discussion, and occasional festival sweets may be acceptable if they are transparent and do not create undue influence.

## GH-016 Vendor and Client Gifts

Vendor and client gifts create conflict-of-interest risk. Employees must apply threshold rules, report as required, and obtain approvals before accepting high-value items.

## GH-017 Procurement Blackout Period

Employees must not accept gifts from vendors during active RFP, contract negotiation, renewal, audit, dispute, or vendor selection without Compliance escalation.

## GH-018 Hospitality Rules

Meals and events must have legitimate business purpose. An Acme employee and the counterparty should generally attend. Travel or lodging paid by a third party requires Compliance approval.

## GH-019 Sponsorships and Donations

Sponsorships and charitable donations requested by clients or vendors require business justification and Compliance review when tied to commercial negotiations.

## GH-020 Refusal and Surrender Process

Employees should politely decline inappropriate gifts when possible. If refusal is impractical or offensive, the employee may accept temporarily, notify Compliance promptly, and surrender or donate the item as instructed.

## GH-021 Repeated Offers and Family Members

Repeated gifts from the same counterparty require Compliance review. Family members may not accept gifts that would be prohibited if offered directly to the employee.

## GH-022 Documentation Requirements

Employees must retain gift value estimates, counterparty identity, business purpose, approvals, and register entries as required by threshold.

## GH-023 Prohibited Actions

Employees must not solicit gifts, disguise cash equivalents as merchandise, or accept gifts to influence procurement or regulatory outcomes.

## GH-024 Exceptions

Exceptions require Compliance and Legal review and must be documented before acceptance.

## GH-025 Audit and Monitoring

Compliance monitors gift register entries, procurement timing, and repeat counterparties through periodic sampling.

### Example: INR 2,000 festival sweets from vendor

Scenario: Vendor sends INR 2,000 festival sweets.  
Policy outcome: Allowed.  
Required approvals: None.  
Required records: Optional note if part of a pattern.  
Why: Below INR 2,500 and modest if infrequent.  
Relevant sections: GH-002, GH-003.

### Example: INR 5,000 gift from vendor

Scenario: Vendor offers a gift worth INR 5,000.  
Policy outcome: Report to Compliance and register.  
Required approvals: Manager awareness recommended.  
Required records: Gift register within 5 business days.  
Why: Exceeds INR 5,000 reporting threshold.  
Relevant sections: GH-003, GH-006, AM-010.

### Example: INR 12,000 gift hamper from vendor

Scenario: Vendor offers a hamper worth INR 12,000.  
Policy outcome: Needs approval before acceptance.  
Required approvals: Manager, Compliance.  
Required records: Gift register and approval record.  
Why: Exceeds INR 10,000 approval threshold.  
Relevant sections: GH-003, GH-006, AM-010.

### Example: INR 3,000 cash gift

Scenario: Vendor offers INR 3,000 cash.  
Policy outcome: Not allowed.  
Required approvals: Compliance escalation.  
Why: Cash and cash equivalents are prohibited.  
Relevant sections: GH-004, AM-010.

### Example: Sports tickets without vendor attendance

Scenario: Vendor offers tickets but does not attend.  
Policy outcome: Needs approval or Escalate depending on value and timing.  
Required approvals: Manager and/or Compliance.  
Why: Hospitality should generally include business attendance and purpose.  
Relevant sections: GH-018, GH-009.

### Example: Dinner with client

Scenario: Employee hosts a working dinner with a client.  
Policy outcome: Allowed with business purpose and expense policy compliance.  
Required approvals: Per meal thresholds in travel policy.  
Relevant sections: GH-018, TE-004.

### Example: Gift to public official

Scenario: Employee wants to give a gift to a public official after a meeting.  
Policy outcome: Escalate.  
Required approvals: Legal, Compliance.  
Why: Public official rules apply before offer or acceptance.  
Relevant sections: GH-007, AM-014.

### Example: Gift during RFP

Scenario: Vendor offers a gift during active RFP.  
Policy outcome: Escalate.  
Required approvals: Compliance.  
Why: Procurement blackout prohibits vendor gifts without escalation.  
Relevant sections: GH-017, GH-009.

### Example: Repeated small gifts

Scenario: Same vendor gives multiple modest gifts in one quarter.  
Policy outcome: Compliance review.  
Required approvals: Compliance.  
Why: Cumulative or repeated offers increase conflict risk.  
Relevant sections: GH-008, GH-021, GH-003.

### Example: Charitable donation requested by client

Scenario: Client asks Acme to make a charitable donation tied to a deal.  
Policy outcome: Needs approval.  
Required approvals: Compliance and business approver.  
Why: Sponsorships and donations require review when commercially connected.  
Relevant sections: GH-019, GH-009.
