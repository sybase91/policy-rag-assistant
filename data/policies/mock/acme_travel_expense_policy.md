# Acme Corp Travel and Expense Policy

Policy Owner: Finance  
Effective Date: 2026-01-01  
Version: 2.0  
Applies To: All Acme Corp employees and contractors on business travel  
Related Policies: Reimbursement Policy, Approval Matrix  
Systems of Record: Acme Expense System, Travel Booking Portal

## TE-001 Purpose

This policy defines reasonable, necessary, and documented travel and business entertainment expenses for Acme Corp employees.

## TE-002 Scope

This policy covers domestic and international business travel, lodging, meals, client entertainment, and related expenses.

## TE-003 General Reimbursement Principles

Expenses must be reasonable, necessary, have a business purpose, use economical options, be documented, and be pre-approved where required by the Approval Matrix.

## TE-004 Client Meal Thresholds

| Client meal amount (INR) | Approval required |
|------------------------|-------------------|
| Up to 10,000 | Manager |
| 10,001–25,000 | Manager + Finance |
| Above 25,000 | Manager + Finance pre-approval before the event |

Client meals require attendee names, business purpose, and itemized receipts.

## TE-005 Booking Channels

Employees must book air, hotel, and rail through approved channels unless Finance approves an exception.

## TE-006 Alcohol on Expense Claims

Alcohol is not reimbursable unless pre-approved by Finance. Alcohol must be itemized separately on the receipt and supported by business purpose.

## TE-007 Domestic Travel

Domestic travel should use economy-class transport unless a pre-approved exception applies.

## TE-008 International Travel

International business travel requires manager and Finance approval before booking.

## TE-009 Air Travel

Economy is the default. Premium economy or business class is allowed only for long-haul routes or role-based pre-approved exceptions. First class is not reimbursable.

## TE-010 Rail Bus and Local Transport

Rail, bus, metro, taxi, and rideshare are reimbursable when reasonable and business-related.

## TE-011 Lodging

Employees should book standard rooms in reasonable business locations. Hotel upgrades require manager pre-approval and Finance approval when incremental cost exceeds INR 10,000.

## TE-012 Employee Meals During Travel

Employee-only meals during travel are reimbursable within per-diem or reasonable actual limits defined by Finance.

## TE-013 Conference and Events

Conference registration and event fees require business purpose and manager approval.

## TE-014 Visas and Passports

Business-required visa and passport fees are reimbursable with manager approval.

## TE-015 Laundry and Incidentals

Laundry is reimbursable for trips longer than 5 nights. Other incidentals must be reasonable and documented.

## TE-016 Internet and Phone

Business internet and phone charges during travel are reimbursable when documented.

## TE-017 Travel Insurance

Travel insurance is reimbursable when required for the destination or approved by Finance.

## TE-018 Cancellations and No-Shows

Cancellation and no-show charges are reimbursable only when unavoidable and manager-approved.

## TE-019 Business and Personal Travel Combined

Personal extensions to business trips are not reimbursable. Airfare must be documented to show business portion when mixed travel occurs.

## TE-020 Family and Guest Travel

Family or guest travel is not reimbursable unless explicitly approved for a business event.

## TE-021 Personal Card Usage

Personal card payment is permitted when policy rules, receipts, and approvals are met. See RE-005 in the Reimbursement Policy.

## TE-022 Required Documentation

Claims require date, amount, currency, business purpose, category, payment method, receipt, approvals, and attendee names where relevant.

## TE-023 Non-Reimbursable Expenses

Non-reimbursable items include personal entertainment, fines, traffic violations, minibar charges, spa or gym, childcare, pet care, luggage upgrades unless pre-approved, and luxury transport.

## TE-024 Escalation

Unusual travel expenses escalate to Finance and the employee's management chain.

## TE-025 Audit and Monitoring

Finance audits travel claims through sampling, duplicate detection, and receipt validation.

## TE-026 Travel Policy Owner

Finance owns this policy and publishes annual updates.

## TE-027 Local Transportation Limits

Local transportation must be reasonable and supported by receipts where required by the Reimbursement Policy.

## TE-028 Mixed Currency Claims

International trips may include multiple currencies; each expense must be documented in original currency.

## TE-029 Sponsored Travel

If a vendor or client sponsors travel, Compliance review is required before acceptance.

## TE-030 Policy Summary

In summary: book through approved channels, document business purpose, obtain meal and upgrade approvals per thresholds, and itemize alcohol separately.

### Example: INR 18,000 client dinner with external guests

Scenario: Employee hosts two external guests and pays with a personal card.  
Policy outcome: Needs approval.  
Required approvals: Manager.  
Required records: Itemized receipt, attendee names, business purpose.  
Why: Amount is in the INR 10,001–25,000 band.  
Relevant sections: TE-004, AM-008.

### Example: INR 30,000 client dinner

Scenario: Employee requests reimbursement for a client dinner above INR 25,000.  
Policy outcome: Needs approval before the event.  
Required approvals: Manager + Finance pre-approval.  
Why: Exceeds INR 25,000 pre-approval threshold.  
Relevant sections: TE-004, AM-008.

### Example: Hotel upgrade

Scenario: Employee books a suite upgrade on a business trip.  
Policy outcome: Needs approval.  
Required approvals: Manager; Finance if incremental cost above INR 10,000.  
Why: Upgrades are not standard lodging.  
Relevant sections: TE-011, AM-008.

### Example: Alcohol on bill

Scenario: Employee submits a client meal receipt that includes alcohol.  
Policy outcome: Needs approval; alcohol may be denied without Finance pre-approval.  
Required approvals: Finance pre-approval for alcohol portion.  
Relevant sections: TE-006, TE-004.

### Example: Lost taxi receipt

Scenario: Employee lost a taxi receipt for a business ride.  
Policy outcome: Needs more information or Needs approval depending on amount.  
Required approvals: Manager; Finance if above INR 2,000.  
Why: Missing receipt rules apply per Reimbursement Policy RE-004.  
Relevant sections: RE-004, AM-009.

### Example: Personal weekend added to business trip

Scenario: Employee extends a trip for personal reasons.  
Policy outcome: Personal portion not reimbursable.  
Why: Mixed travel personal costs are excluded.  
Relevant sections: TE-019, TE-023.

### Example: Flight upgrade

Scenario: Employee upgrades to business class on a short domestic flight.  
Policy outcome: Not allowed unless pre-approved exception.  
Why: Economy is default for domestic travel.  
Relevant sections: TE-009, TE-023.

### Example: International booking outside approved channel

Scenario: Employee books international travel outside the portal.  
Policy outcome: Needs approval or may be denied.  
Required approvals: Manager + Finance.  
Relevant sections: TE-005, TE-008.
