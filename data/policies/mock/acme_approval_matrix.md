# Acme Corp Approval Matrix

Policy Owner: Finance, People Operations, Legal, Compliance, and Information Security  
Effective Date: 2026-01-01  
Version: 2.0  
Applies To: All Acme Corp employees, contractors, managers, approvers, and delegated approvers  
Related Policies: Travel and Expense, Reimbursement, Gifts and Hospitality, Remote Work, Data Access  
Systems of Record: Acme Workflow, Expense System, Gift Register, Access Management Portal

## AM-001 Purpose

This matrix defines approval authority across Finance, HR, Legal, Compliance, Information Security, and business management for common workplace requests. It is the cross-functional routing reference for Acme Corp policies.

## AM-002 Scope

This matrix applies to employees, contractors, managers, approvers, and delegated approvers worldwide, unless a local addendum specifies a stricter path.

## AM-003 Approval Principles

- Approvals must be documented before action where reasonably possible.
- An approver cannot approve their own request.
- The approver must have sufficient authority for the risk, amount, and data classification involved.
- Approvals must be retained in the system of record.
- Higher-risk items require functional review (Finance, Legal, Compliance, HR, or InfoSec).

## AM-004 Segregation of Duties

Employees cannot approve their own expenses, gifts, data access, vendor access, or policy exceptions. Managers cannot bypass Finance, Legal, Compliance, HR, or Information Security where those functions are required by policy.

## AM-005 Delegated Authority

Managers may delegate approval authority during planned absence. Delegation must be documented in the workflow system, time-bound, and limited to the delegate's authority level. Sensitive approvals (Legal, Compliance, InfoSec) should not be delegated without policy owner consent.

## AM-006 Emergency Approval

Emergency approvals may be verbal only when immediate business continuity, safety, or customer impact is at stake. Verbal approval must be documented in the system of record within 2 business days.

## AM-007 Retrospective Approval

Post-facto approval is exceptional and may be rejected. Repeated retrospective approval requests are escalated to Finance or the relevant policy owner.

## AM-008 Travel and Expense Approval Matrix

| Request type | Approval required |
|--------------|-------------------|
| Local transport under INR 2,000 | Manager |
| Local transport INR 2,000–10,000 | Manager |
| Client meal up to INR 10,000 | Manager |
| Client meal INR 10,001–25,000 | Manager + Finance |
| Client meal above INR 25,000 | Manager + Finance pre-approval |
| Alcohol on expense claim | Finance pre-approval |
| Hotel upgrade | Manager pre-approval; Finance if incremental cost above INR 10,000 |
| International business travel | Manager + Finance |
| Non-standard travel class | Manager + Finance + VP |

| Exception type | Approval required |
|----------------|-------------------|

| Missing receipt above INR 10,000 | Finance + Business VP |
| Late claim 31–60 days | Manager |
| Late claim 61–90 days | Manager + Finance |
| Late claim over 90 days | Finance exception only |

## AM-010 Gifts and Hospitality Approval Matrix

| Request type | Approval / outcome |
|--------------|-------------------|
| Gift under INR 2,500, not vendor/public official/cash | No approval; record optional |
| Gift INR 2,500–5,000 | Manager awareness recommended |
| Gift above INR 5,000 | Compliance reporting |
| Gift above INR 10,000 | Manager + Compliance approval before acceptance |
| Cash or cash equivalent | Not allowed; Compliance escalation |
| Public official | Legal + Compliance pre-approval |
| Gift during procurement period | Compliance escalation |

## AM-012 Data Access Approval Matrix

| Request type | Approval required |
|--------------|-------------------|
| Internal data | Manager or data owner |
| Confidential data | Data owner + Manager |
| Restricted data | Data owner + InfoSec |
| Customer data external sharing | Business owner + Legal + InfoSec |
| HR data external sharing | HR data owner + Legal + InfoSec |
| Finance data access | Finance data owner |

| Break-glass access | InfoSec + system owner; post-review within 1 business day |

## AM-013 Vendor and Third-Party Access

Vendor access to data, systems, production environments, analytics extracts, and AI tools requires business justification, data owner approval, vendor security review, and InfoSec approval for Restricted or customer data.

## AM-014 Public Official and Government Interaction Approvals

Legal and Compliance approval is required for gifts, hospitality, travel support, sponsorships, donations, or anything of value involving public officials or government entities.

## AM-015 Approval Evidence

Acceptable evidence includes workflow approval, ticket approval, email approval from an authorized approver, signed exception record, or compliance register entry.

## AM-016 Expiration of Approvals

Approvals expire if facts materially change, the amount increases, the counterparty changes, or action is delayed by more than 30 days.

## AM-017 Conflicts and Overrides

When policies conflict, use the stricter approval path. Higher-risk policy requirements override lower-risk guidance.

## AM-018 Escalation

Unresolved approval disputes escalate to the policy owner team listed in the relevant domain policy (Finance, HR, Legal, Compliance, or Information Security).

## AM-019 Examples

### Example: INR 18,000 client dinner

Scenario: Employee hosts two external clients and pays with a personal card.  
Policy outcome: Needs approval.  
Required approvals: Manager.  
Why: Client meal is within the INR 10,000–25,000 band requiring manager approval and Finance may review documentation.  
Relevant sections: AM-008, TE-004.

### Example: INR 30,000 client dinner

Scenario: Employee requests reimbursement for a client dinner above INR 25,000.  
Policy outcome: Needs approval before the event.  
Required approvals: Manager + Finance pre-approval.  
Why: Amount exceeds INR 25,000 pre-approval threshold.  
Relevant sections: AM-008, TE-004.

### Example: INR 12,000 vendor gift

Scenario: Vendor offers a gift hamper worth INR 12,000.  
Policy outcome: Needs approval before acceptance.  
Required approvals: Manager + Compliance.  
Why: Vendor gift exceeds INR 10,000 approval threshold.  
Relevant sections: AM-010, GH-003.

### Example: INR 5,000 vendor gift

Scenario: Vendor offers a modest gift worth INR 5,000.  
Policy outcome: Report to Compliance and register; approval not required before acceptance unless other risk factors apply.  
Required approvals: Compliance reporting.  
Why: Gift exceeds INR 5,000 reporting threshold.  
Relevant sections: AM-010, GH-003, GH-006.

### Example: Cash gift from vendor

Scenario: Vendor offers INR 3,000 in cash.  
Policy outcome: Not allowed.  
Required approvals: Compliance escalation.  
Why: Cash and cash equivalents are prohibited.  
Relevant sections: AM-010, GH-004.

### Example: Two weeks remote work with manager approval

Scenario: Employee works remotely for two weeks with documented manager approval.  
Policy outcome: Allowed with documented approval.  
Required approvals: Manager (already obtained).  
Why: Duration falls in the 6–20 business day band.  
Relevant sections: AM-011, RW-003.

### Example: Cross-border remote work one month

Scenario: Employee requests to work from another country for one month.  
Policy outcome: Escalate / Needs approval.  
Required approvals: HR + Legal + Tax + InfoSec.  
Why: Cross-border remote work requires advance multi-functional review.  
Relevant sections: AM-011, RW-005.

### Example: Customer data to external vendor

Scenario: Team wants to share customer data with an analytics vendor.  
Policy outcome: Needs approval.  
Required approvals: Business owner + Legal + InfoSec.  
Why: External customer data sharing requires security and legal review.  
Relevant sections: AM-012, DA-007.

## AM-020 Audit and Monitoring

Approvals are subject to audit sampling, exception reporting, and periodic control testing by Internal Audit, Finance, Compliance, and Information Security.
