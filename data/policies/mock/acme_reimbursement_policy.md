# Acme Corp Reimbursement Policy

Policy Owner: Finance  
Effective Date: 2026-01-01  
Version: 2.0  
Applies To: All Acme Corp employees and contractors submitting expense claims  
Related Policies: Travel and Expense Policy, Approval Matrix  
Systems of Record: Acme Expense System

## RE-001 Purpose

This policy defines how employees submit, document, and receive reimbursement for legitimate business expenses under accountable-plan principles.

## RE-002 Scope

This policy covers reimbursement claims for travel, meals, supplies, and other business expenses not paid directly by Acme Corp.

## RE-003 Accountable Reimbursement Principles

Reimbursements require business connection, substantiation, timely submission, and return of unused advances where applicable.

## RE-004 Missing Receipts

| Missing receipt amount (INR) | Requirement |
|------------------------------|-------------|
| Under 2,000 | Lost receipt declaration + Manager approval |
| 2,001–10,000 | Proof of payment + Manager + Finance approval |
| Above 10,000 | Exceptional Finance review; may be denied |

Lost-receipt claims need alternate documentation such as card statement, booking confirmation, or affidavit as Finance requires.

## RE-005 Personal Payment Methods

Employees may use personal card, UPI, or cash when business-appropriate. Employees bear foreign-exchange or late-payment fees unless business-approved. Corporate card should be used where issued.

## RE-006 Submission Timeline

Standard submission deadline is 30 days from expense date. Late claims require escalating approval as defined in the Approval Matrix.

## RE-007 Required Documentation

Claims must include date, amount, currency, business purpose, expense category, payment method, receipt, approvals, and attendee names where relevant.

## RE-008 Receipt Thresholds

Receipts are required for all travel, lodging, client meals, and expenses above INR 1,000. Itemized receipts are required for meals and client entertainment.

## RE-009 Corporate Card vs Personal Card

Corporate card is preferred for travel and high-volume expenses. Personal card claims follow the same substantiation and approval rules.

## RE-010 Advances

Travel advances must be reconciled within the standard submission timeline. Unused advance balances must be returned.

## RE-011 Duplicate Claims

Duplicate or overlapping claims are prohibited and subject to Finance review and disciplinary action.

## RE-012 Currency Conversion

Foreign currency claims must use the expense system exchange rate or supported receipt conversion method.

## RE-013 Tax Treatment

Employees are responsible for understanding local tax implications of reimbursements where applicable. Finance provides guidance on taxable benefits.

## RE-014 Expense Categories

Valid categories include travel, meals, lodging, client entertainment, supplies, and approved professional fees.

## RE-015 Non-Reimbursable Items

Personal expenses, fines, gifts outside policy, and non-approved upgrades are not reimbursable.

## RE-016 Exceptions

Exceptions require Finance approval and documented business justification.

## RE-017 Audit and Fraud

Finance audits claims for fraud indicators, duplicate submissions, and policy violations.

## RE-018 Repeated Late or Missing Claims

Repeated late or missing-receipt claims escalate to Finance and may affect future claim eligibility.

## RE-019 Late Submission Bands

Claims submitted 31–60 days late require manager approval. Claims 61–90 days late require manager and Finance approval. Claims over 90 days late require exceptional Finance review.

## RE-020 Partial Reimbursement

Finance may reimburse only the business-supported portion of a claim when personal items are mixed on one receipt.

## RE-021 Escalation

Disputed denials escalate to Finance operations and the employee's management chain.

## RE-022 Systems of Record

All approvals and claims must be recorded in the Acme Expense System.

## RE-023 Manager Responsibilities

Managers must verify business purpose and policy compliance before approving claims.

## RE-024 Prohibited Actions

Employees must not split receipts to avoid thresholds, alter receipts, or submit personal expenses as business costs.

## RE-025 Audit and Monitoring

Finance performs periodic sampling of receipts, approvals, and duplicate detection.

### Example: Lost taxi receipt

Scenario: Employee lost a taxi receipt for a INR 1,500 business ride.  
Policy outcome: Needs approval.  
Required approvals: Manager.  
Required records: Lost receipt declaration.  
Why: Under INR 2,000 missing-receipt threshold.  
Relevant sections: RE-004, AM-009.

### Example: Personal card client dinner

Scenario: Employee paid a client dinner with a personal card.  
Policy outcome: Needs approval per travel meal thresholds.  
Required approvals: Manager; Finance if above INR 10,000.  
Relevant sections: RE-005, TE-004.

### Example: Late claim after 45 days

Scenario: Employee submits a claim 45 days after expense date.  
Policy outcome: Needs approval.  
Required approvals: Manager.  
Why: Falls in 31–60 day late submission band.  
Relevant sections: RE-006, RE-019, AM-009.

### Example: Late claim after 100 days

Scenario: Employee submits a claim more than 90 days late.  
Policy outcome: Exceptional Finance review; may be denied.  
Required approvals: Finance exception.  
Relevant sections: RE-019, AM-009.

### Example: Duplicate claim

Scenario: Employee submits the same hotel charge twice.  
Policy outcome: Denied pending Finance review.  
Relevant sections: RE-011, RE-017.

### Example: Foreign currency claim

Scenario: Employee submits a USD hotel receipt.  
Policy outcome: Allowed with substantiation.  
Required records: Receipt and system conversion.  
Relevant sections: RE-012, RE-007.

### Example: Partial reimbursement

Scenario: Employee submits a meal receipt with personal items.  
Policy outcome: Partial reimbursement of business portion only.  
Relevant sections: RE-020, RE-007.

### Example: Missing itemized receipt

Scenario: Employee submits a credit card slip without itemization for a client meal.  
Policy outcome: Needs more information.  
Why: Itemized receipt required for client entertainment.  
Relevant sections: RE-008, TE-004.
